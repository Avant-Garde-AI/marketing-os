# Avant-Garde — Voice & Tone

The brand speaks with the confidence of a practitioner, not the enthusiasm of a marketer. Everything we write should feel like it was written by someone who has already done the thing — not someone trying to sell it.

---

## The Voice in Three Words

**Precise. Confident. Understated.**

---

## Core Voice Attributes

### Precise
- Specific > vague. Numbers, categories, status labels.
- "We build, scale, and open-source AI-native digital ventures." (Not: "We create innovative AI solutions.")
- Short sentences. Declarative. Period.

### Confident
- We don't hedge. We don't use "try" or "might" or "could potentially."
- "The future of commerce isn't just automated. It's autonomous, elegant, and intelligent."
- State facts and beliefs as facts and beliefs.

### Understated
- The brand doesn't shout. It doesn't use exclamation marks in headlines.
- Never oversell. Let the work speak.
- Gold is used sparingly. So is superlative language.

---

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Declarative, weighty | "Agentic Commerce, In Practice." |
| Eyebrow label | Categorical, factual | "Active Ventures" / "Our Methodology" |
| Body copy | Clear, conversational | "Most venture studios keep their methods proprietary. We do the opposite." |
| CTA | Direct, inviting | "Start Building." / "Get in Touch." |
| Manifesto | Philosophical, editorial | "Build in public. Share everything." |
| Navigation | Minimal, functional | Single words: Ventures, Playbooks, About |
| Metrics/stats | Matter-of-fact | "$2.4M ARR" / "100% Open Source" |
| Sign-offs | Warm, signature | "Avant-Garde." (in Pinyon Script) |

## Headline Patterns

Headlines use a distinctive mixed structure: **roman + italic** in a single line. The italic is typically the emotional or poetic word — the one that earns the sentence.

**Structure: [Direct statement] + *[italic poetic word]* + [period]**

Examples:
- "Build **Forward.**" → `Build <i>Forward.</i>`
- "Built with **AI-First** DNA." → `Built with <i>AI-First</i> DNA.`
- "Agentic ***Commerce***, In Practice." → `Agentic <i>Commerce</i>, In Practice.`
- "Join our ***mission.***" → `Join our <i>mission.</i>`
- "Ready to build ***with agents?***"

**Rules:**
- The italic word is always meaningful, not decorative — it carries the emotional weight
- Headlines almost never end with exclamation marks
- Period at the end of statements, even when no grammatically required (enforces rhythm)
- Headline fragments are acceptable and often preferred ("In Practice." not "This Is In Practice.")

## Eyebrow Labels

Short uppercase labels that categorize content. Always in gold. Always small. Always sparse.

**Rules:**
- 1–3 words maximum
- ALL CAPS, tracking widest
- Functional categories, not marketing language
  - **Good:** "Active Ventures" / "Our Philosophy" / "Documentation" / "GitHub"
  - **Bad:** "Explore Our Amazing Portfolio" / "Discover What We Believe"
- Often paired with a gold rule line before it

---

## Copy Rules

**Do:**
- Open sentences with the strongest word (verb or concrete noun)
- Use the Oxford comma
- Use em-dashes (—) for parenthetical statements, not hyphens
- Use numerals for all numbers (12, not "twelve"), except at the start of a sentence
- Write "AI-native" with a hyphen and capital AI
- Write "open-source" as a hyphenated adjective, "open source" as a noun
- Use "In Practice" (capitalized) as a brand term meaning: deployed, proven, real
- Prefer active voice

**Don't:**
- Use buzzwords: "revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changing," "synergy," "leverage" (as a verb)
- Write "cutting-edge" or "state-of-the-art"
- Use ellipsis (...) in headlines or UI
- Use more than one exclamation mark anywhere in a given app/page
- Write in second person for manifesto/philosophy content (use "we")

## Vocabulary

| Preferred | Avoid |
|---|---|
| AI-native | AI-powered, AI-driven |
| Agentic | Automated (when referring to agents) |
| Ventures | Startups, projects |
| Playbooks | Guides (only), tutorials |
| In Practice | Live, deployed, production (in brand contexts) |
| Build | Create, develop (too generic) |
| Open-source | Free (when describing open-source) |
| Studio | Agency, firm |
| Compound | Stack, layer |
| Autonomous | Automatic |

## Numbers & Stats

- Display stats in Playfair Display, large (3xl–5xl), no leading zeroes
- Always pair a stat with a label in uppercase tracking-widest below it
- Format: `24K+` / `$2.4M ARR` / `100%` / `47` (no filler words in the number itself)
- Stat labels: "Active Ventures" / "GitHub Stars" / "Open Repos" / "Contributors"

---

## The Script Sign-off

"Avant-Garde." written in Pinyon Script at a reduced opacity is the brand's signature. Use it:
- At the end of philosophy/manifesto copy blocks
- As a decorative element in dark sections
- In the footer

Never spell it out in regular type in this context — it must always be in the script font and at `opacity: 60–80%`.

---

## What We Are Not

- Not playful or casual (no emojis in product UI, no "Hey!" greetings)
- Not aggressive or salesy (no urgency tactics, no countdown timers)
- Not overly formal or corporate (no "solutions," no "leverage synergies")
- Not academic (no passive voice, no jargon walls)
- Not humble (no "we hope to," "we try to")

---

## Example: Before/After Rewrite

**Before (generic):**
> "Our innovative AI-powered platform helps businesses leverage cutting-edge automation to drive growth and improve efficiency across all their operations."

**After (Avant-Garde voice):**
> "We build, scale, and open-source AI-native digital ventures. Our playbooks are public. Our tools are free."
